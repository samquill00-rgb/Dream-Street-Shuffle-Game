⟡ LORE: Watkins ⟡

Bernard Watkins came to Soho in 1962 with nothing but a suitcase full of manuscripts and a letter of introduction to a man who'd been dead for three years.

He made his name writing about the places nobody else dared go — the back rooms, the after-hours clubs, the conversations that happened between midnight and dawn.

They say he never wrote sober. They say a lot of things about Bernard Watkins.
