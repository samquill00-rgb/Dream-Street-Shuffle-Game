Dream Street Shuffle

Rain lingers in puddles, crosshatched with moonshining neurons, long after the afternoon's gone.

In your bag, you have your novel in manuscript. You need to sell it, pronto.

Dean Street after midnight. The Colony is somewhere above you. Somewhere below is the cellar where men go to lose things.

You've been walking all night. The book is heavy. Your confidence is a thing you'd rather not examine too closely at this hour.

The street is quiet now, or quieter than before. There's a man outside the French who might be anyone. The lamp above him does not flicker but looks as though it might.
